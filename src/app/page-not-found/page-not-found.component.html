Holy crow!

Looks like the page you were looking for has been stolen! Please try another page, or stick around and read my 10 favorite crow facts:

  1. They have the largest brain to body ratio of all birds, and the anatomy of their brain is similar to that of humans. This results in intelligence that rivals our own.
  2. Crows are one of the most resourceful creatures. They are able to not only use, but create their own tools, even if they have never seen it before. These include but are not limited to knives and hooks.
  3. When they encounter a tough nut that they can't crack, they have a few methods they like to use. If there's a road nearby, they'll place it in the path of a car (they prefer smaller cars so the nut isn't reduced to a pulp), and will wait for the traffic signal to change before they retrieve it. If no cars are around, they know just how high they need to drop it from to let gravity do the work without wasting energy by flying too high.
  4. They have discovered that they can use breadcrumbs for fishing.
  5. Crows can mimic sounds even better than parrots can. They even use this to their advantage in the wild. If they find a dead animal with skin to thick for their beak to break, they will mimic the call of a bigger animal to lure them to the location. Once the animal has left, the crow will pick at the remains.
  6. They also use their ability to mimic sounds to slightly alter their dialect to differentiate their community from others.
  7. They can analyze and communicate so effectively that generations later, the young crows can recognize what was described in the legends incredibly accurately. They often tell stories of humans who have mistreated them in this way, and if the human ever returns, the entire group will attack.
  8. Crows also can accurately judge the character of a human by expression and slight facial features. If they trust the human enough, they can even bond with them and share what they judge as valuable.
  9. They often hold funerals for their dead. If they find a dying crow, they'll kill it so it doesn't suffer (which is why a group of crows is called a murder).
  10. Crows choose a mate for life. Even though they reach sexual maturity after 2 years, they often wait until they find the perfect partner.
